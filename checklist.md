# EARCLE Preflight Checklist

This checklist is intended for health-adjacent conversational flows such as screening and psychoeducation.

## 1. Consent

1. Is consent requested before collecting sensitive information?
2. Is consent specific about what is collected, why, and for how long?
3. Is participation clearly voluntary and can the user stop at any time?
4. Are contact and support resources provided where appropriate?

## 2. Data minimization

1. Is each data field necessary for the stated purpose?
2. Are optional fields clearly marked optional?
3. Is there a clear statement about what is not collected?

## 3. Purpose and retention

1. Is purpose stated in plain language near the point of collection?
2. Is retention stated in plain language with a concrete time window?
3. Is deletion or revocation described in plain language?

## 4. Medical and safety disclaimers

1. Does the flow avoid claiming diagnosis?
2. Does it provide an appropriate disclaimer for medical context?
3. Does it include guidance for urgent or crisis situations if relevant?

## 5. Uncertainty and limits

1. Does the system acknowledge uncertainty where appropriate?
2. Does it avoid overconfident conclusions?
3. Does it explain what the result means and does not mean?

## 6. Harm reduction and escalation

1. Does the flow detect high-risk content where applicable?
2. Does it provide safe responses and escalation pathways?
3. Does it avoid giving instructions that could cause harm?

## 7. Privacy and security

1. Are access controls and roles defined for any stored data?
2. Are identifiers minimized or avoided?
3. Are logs and exports controlled?

## 8. Auditability

1. Can a reviewer reconstruct what happened and why?
2. Are key decisions logged with reasons?
3. Is there a clear trail from consent to data use to outputs?
